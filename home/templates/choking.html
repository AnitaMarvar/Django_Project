Choking occurs when an object or food becomes lodged in the throat or airway, blocking airflow. This can be life-threatening and requires immediate attention. Understanding how to recognize and respond to choking can save lives.

Signs of Choking

Mild Choking

Coughing or gagging
Difficulty breathing or speaking

Severe Choking

Inability to cough, speak, or breathe
Clutching the throat
Bluish skin color (cyanosis)
Loss of consciousness



First Aid for Choking


For Conscious Adults and Children Over 1 Year:

Step 1: Encourage the person to cough if they can. Coughing can help expel the object.
Step 2: If coughing doesn’t help, perform the Heimlich maneuver:
Stand behind the person.
Place your arms around their waist.
Place a fist above their navel and cover it with your other hand.
Deliver quick, inward, and upward thrusts.
Step 3: Call emergency services if the obstruction is not cleared or if the person loses consciousness.

For Infants Under 1 Year:

Step 1: Place the infant face down on your forearm, supporting their head and neck.
Step 2: Give up to 5 firm back blows between the shoulder blades using the heel of your hand.
Step 3: If the object is not dislodged, turn the infant face up and give up to 5 chest thrusts using two fingers in the center of the chest.
Step 4: Call emergency services if the obstruction persists or the infant becomes unconscious.



When to you Seek Medical Help


Persistent Symptoms: If the person continues to have difficulty breathing or coughing after the object is expelled.
Unconsciousness: If the person becomes unconscious, call emergency services immediately.
Injury or Trauma: If the person has sustained injury from the choking incident.




Prevention Tips


Proper Eating Habits

Chew food thoroughly and avoid talking or laughing while eating.

Avoiding Choking Hazards

Keep small objects and food items that could pose a choking risk away from young children.

First Aid Training

Take a first aid course to learn proper techniques for dealing with choking and other emergencies.



FAQS

What should I do if the Heimlich maneuver doesn’t work?

Continue the Heimlich maneuver and seek emergency medical help immediately.


Can choking happen with liquids?

Yes, choking can occur if liquids are inhaled into the airway. Follow the same steps for treatment and seek medical help if necessary.


Is it safe to perform the Heimlich maneuver on pregnant women?

Yes, perform chest thrusts instead of abdominal thrusts. Place your hands higher on the chest and avoid pressing on the abdomen.
