Essential First Aid Tips

Quick and Lifesaving Advice for Everyday Emergencies


How to Stop Bleeding:
Apply Pressure: Use a clean cloth to apply firm pressure on the wound. Hold the pressure until the bleeding stops.
Elevate the Injury: Raise the injured area above heart level if possible, to slow the bleeding.
Don’t Remove the Cloth: If blood soaks through the cloth, place another one on top instead of removing the original one.


Treating Minor Burns:
Cool the Burn: Run cool (not cold) water over the burn for at least 10 minutes.
Cover with a Clean Cloth: Protect the burn with a sterile gauze or clean cloth, but avoid using fluffy cotton or adhesive bandages.
Avoid Ice: Do not apply ice directly to the burn as it can cause further damage to the tissue.


Handling a Sprain:
R.I.C.E. Method: Rest the injured area, Ice it for 15-20 minutes, Compress with a bandage, and Elevate it above heart level.
Avoid Weight: Keep weight off the injured area to prevent further damage.
Seek Medical Attention: If there’s severe pain or swelling, consult a healthcare professional.


CPR Basics:
Check Responsiveness: Gently shake the person and shout to see if they respond.
Call for Help: If the person is unresponsive, call emergency services immediately.
Start Chest Compressions: Place your hands in the center of the chest and push hard and fast at a rate of 100-120 compressions per minute.


Choking Relief:
Encourage Coughing: If the person is able to speak or cough, encourage them to continue coughing to clear the airway.
Perform Abdominal Thrusts (Heimlich Maneuver): Stand behind the person, place a fist above their navel, and thrust inward and upward to try to expel the object.
Back Blows: If thrusts don’t work, give firm back blows between the shoulder blades with the heel of your hand.

Step-by-Step Tutorials

Thorough Instructions to Act with Confidence


How to Perform CPR:
Step 1: Check the Scene and the Person Ensure the area is safe, then tap and shout to check if the person is conscious.
Step 2: Call for Help: If unresponsive, call emergency services or instruct someone else to do so.
Step 3: Open the Airway: Tilt the head back and lift the chin.
Step 4: Check for Breathing: Look, listen, and feel for breathing for no more than 10 seconds.
Step 5: Begin Chest Compressions: Place the heel of one hand on the center of the chest, place the other hand on top, and press down hard and fast at a depth of about 2 inches.
Step 6: Give Rescue Breaths: If trained, give two rescue breaths after 30 compressions by pinching the nose shut, covering the mouth with yours, and blowing until the chest rises.


Treating a Burn:
Step 1: Assess the Burn: Determine if it’s a first-degree (superficial), second-degree (partial thickness), or third-degree (full thickness) burn.
Step 2: Cool the Burn: For first- and second-degree burns, run cool water over the area for 10-15 minutes.
Step 3: Protect the Burn: Cover with a clean, non-stick cloth or sterile gauze.
Step 4: Manage Pain: Use over-the-counter pain relievers as needed.
Step 5: Monitor for Infection: Watch for signs of infection, such as increased redness, swelling, or pus.


How to Treat a Cut or Laceration:
Step 1: Stop the Bleeding: Apply pressure with a clean cloth or bandage until the bleeding stops.
Step 2: Clean the Wound: Rinse the cut with clean water to remove debris. Use soap around, but not inside, the wound.
Step 3: Apply Antibiotic Ointment: Spread a thin layer of antibiotic ointment to reduce the risk of infection.
Step 4: Bandage the Wound: Cover with a sterile bandage or adhesive dressing.
Step 5: Watch for Infection: Check daily for signs of infection, such as redness, warmth, or swelling.


Managing a Sprained Ankle:
Step 1: Rest the Ankle:Avoid putting weight on the injured ankle for at least 24-48 hours.
Step 2: Ice the Ankle: Apply an ice pack wrapped in a cloth for 15-20 minutes every 2-3 hours during the first 48 hours.
Step 3: Compress the Ankle:Use an elastic bandage to wrap the ankle, providing support but not cutting off circulation.
Step 4: Elevate the Ankle: Keep the ankle elevated above the level of the heart as much as possible to reduce swelling.
Step 5: Gradual Return to Activity:Once pain and swelling have reduced, gradually reintroduce movement and weight-bearing activities.


Handling a Nosebleed:
Step 1: Stay Calm and Lean Forward: Sit down and lean slightly forward to avoid swallowing blood.
Step 2: Pinch the Nostrils:Use your thumb and index finger to pinch the nostrils shut, breathing through the mouth.
Step 3: Hold for 10 Minutes:Continue pinching for at least 10 minutes, then release and check if the bleeding has stopped.
Step 4: Apply a Cold Compress: Place a cold compress or ice pack on the bridge of the nose to help constrict blood vessels.
Step 5: Avoid Nose Picking or Blowing:Refrain from picking or blowing the nose for a few hours to prevent re-bleeding.

When to Seek Medical Help

If the injury is severe, involves heavy bleeding, or if you are unsure of the condition, seek professional medical help immediately.
